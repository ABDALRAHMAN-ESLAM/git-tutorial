Ahmed Khaled Tawfik

Who Made the youth read

Ahmed Khaled Tawfik (1962-2018) was an Egyptian writer, known as "The Godfather" of Arab horror and sci-fi, inspiring many with works like "Ma Wara'a Al-Tabi'a."

Radwa Ashour

The Writer of Generations

Radwa Ashour (1946-2014) was a renowned Egyptian writer, known for works like "The Granada Trilogy" that addressed Arab identity and social issues.

Naguib Mahfouz

Naguib Mahfouz (1911-2006), Egyptian writer and Nobel laureate (1988), portrayed Egyptian society in works like "The Cairo Trilogy".

Nabil Farouk

Nabil Farouk (1956-2020) was an Egyptian writer known for his detective and science fiction series, such as "The Impossible Man".

Khaoula Hamdi

Khaoula Hamdi, Tunisian author and professor, is known for her novel "In My Heart is A Hebrew Woman" exploring cultural themes.
